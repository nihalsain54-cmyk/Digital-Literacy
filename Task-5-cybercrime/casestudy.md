# Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick
people into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank
details, or OTPs. It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or
websites that look similar to trusted organizations like banks, online
services, or universities.

A typical phishing attack often happens step by step. First, the
attacker sends a fake email or message pretending to be from a trusted
source, such as a bank or an online payment service. The message usually
creates urgency, for example saying that the user's account will be
blocked if they do not verify their information. Next, the message
includes a link that leads to a fake website that looks almost identical
to the real one. When the victim enters their login details, password,
or OTP on the fake website, the attacker captures that information.
After getting access, the attacker may log into the victim's real
account and misuse it, such as transferring money or stealing personal
data.

Students, elderly people, and individuals who are not very familiar with
online security are often common targets of phishing attacks. The
consequences can include financial loss, identity theft, or unauthorized
access to personal accounts.

To avoid phishing attacks, people should always check the sender's email
address, avoid clicking suspicious links, and never share passwords or
OTPs. Using two-factor authentication and verifying information directly
from official websites can also help prevent such crimes.
