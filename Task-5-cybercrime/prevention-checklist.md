# Stay Safe Online -- Prevention Checklist (For College Students in India)

## General Online Safety

-   **Use strong and unique passwords** for different accounts and
    enable two-factor authentication whenever possible.
-   **Do not click suspicious links** in emails, SMS, or social media
    messages, especially if they create urgency.
-   **Verify websites before entering personal information** by checking
    the official domain and HTTPS security.
-   **Avoid using public Wi‑Fi for sensitive activities** like logging
    into bank accounts or entering passwords.
-   **Keep your devices updated** with the latest software updates and
    antivirus protection.
-   **Limit the personal information you share online**, including your
    address, phone number, and ID documents.

## Financial / UPI Safety

-   **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank details** with anyone, even
    if they claim to be from the bank or payment service.
-   **Remember that receiving money on UPI does not require entering a
    PIN**---if an app asks for a PIN to "receive" money, it is likely a
    scam.
-   **Double‑check the receiver's name and UPI ID before sending money**
    to avoid sending it to the wrong person.

## Reporting Cybercrime

-   **Report online fraud immediately** through the National Cyber Crime
    Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
-   **Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930** as soon as possible if you
    suspect financial fraud or unauthorized transactions.
